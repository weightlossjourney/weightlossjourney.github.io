07/22/2018

Bariatric Surgery and Kidney Stones


While I have heard a lot of horror stories, personally I have never in my life suffered from kidney stones. Until this month...

I know some studies show that people that have had bariatric surgery are at a higher risk of developing kidney stones. However with so many other things to focus on the warning fell to the back of my mind never to be heard from again until this month.

I was relaxing at home one weekend when I started to develop a slight discomfort in my right groin. Within a matter of minutes it had moved up around my belt line and became excruciating. For two hours I walked the floor. The pain so bad I was unable to sit, walk or lay down for any length of time. Then as quickly as it started it went away.

I made an appointment with my doctor for later in the week and explained what happened. While he agreed with me that it sounded like kidney stones he offered no hints or ideas on how to prevent them from happening again. He took blood and urine sample said he would send them off for testing and I never heard from him again about it.

A week or so later I was asleep on a Tuesday night when I awoke in the night with another slight pain but this time in my left groin. Within minutes I was pacing the floor. After two hours of this I woke my dad and told him to drive me to the emergency room. I arrived at the emergency room and checked in with the counter and started pacing. I have been here before and had to wait several hours before they could get to me but they called me back in less that five minutes.

I explained to the doctor what happened and the first words out of his mouth was "Kidney Stones". He could tell I was hurting as I was covered in sweat and my blood pressure was way too high. He stepped out and a few minutes later a nurse came in with a needle and shot me up. Turns out it was Morphine. Let me say I can easily understand how someone could become addicted to the stuff. The pain levels immediately dropped to a more manageable level and I asked the nurse to marry me. She laugh and told me she gets asked that a lot.

After some time I was taken and given a CT scan and was told that I do have kidney Stone and a lot of them. However they were all small enough that the doctor felt there would be no need to perform any kind of procedure as most of them would break down into sand I would never notice as they left the body. He gave me two prescriptions one for pain if I have another attack and one for antibiotics.

I left the hospital around dawn and while I was still sore and in some minor pain it was nowhere near the levels that caused me to go there in the first place. I am still sore several days later but have not had another attack. Let me say this stuff hurts. It hurts in a way I would never wish on anyone and I really hope you never have to go through it.

If you are considering bariatric surgery for weight loss remember that you may be at a higher risk for kidney stones. Make sure you stay up on your fluid intake and talk with your doctor about it. Especially if you have a history of developing them already.

07/04/2018

Medical Bills


With my surgery being a little over six weeks ago I am starting to get invoices from the insurance company on what bills have been submitted and paid for and what I am still responsible for paying.

So far it has been at what I expected but I did receive a little surprise this past Monday in the mail. It was a statement from the insurance in regards to the anesthesiologist. The bill itself was kinda shocking because if this is what they charge I am in the wrong business.

The statement showed the billed amount, how much was covered by insurance, (In this case zero dollars), and how much I was responsible for. The bill was $5,000 US and insurance was going to cover zero dollars and I would be responsible for the full amount of $5,000 dollars.

The first question that came to mind was what did he give me that was worth $5,000 dollars? Especially as the surgery was only about an hour long.

The second question was why did insurance deny covering any part of that bill? Did they think that anesthesia was unnecessary?

It was too late in the afternoon to call as they were already closed for the day so I took the bill with me to work the next day and called my insurance company to see if I could get an answer. I was betting that is was a mistake, as most times insurance refuses to pay it is on something they feel is unnecessary for the procedure in question.

I called and spoke with Kelly who was very helpful and she was just as confused as I as to why it was not paid. Her records show the invoice was received but the computer billing system rejected it and assigned me 100% responsibility on that bill.

She admitted it had to be some kind of mistake and resubmitted the bill for review and overrode the automated computer rejection and said I would get a new statement in 7 to 10 business days. She was unable to tell me if the bill would be rejected again or how much would be covered by insurance.

Take this to heart folks. While I am still waiting on the final judgment on this bill, when you have any procedure done that will be billed to insurance please pay close attention to all your statements and if something looks wrong or strange don't hesitate to give someone a call.

If you do so and remain calm and polite they will do the same and many times will work to help you out. Not every company is out to screw you over but, always write down names, times and what was discussed and you can go a long way in getting things resolved with as little stress as possible.

I still want to know what he did that was worth 5 grand for an hours worth of work though.

03/29/2018

Unhelpful Comments


It amazes me how many people feel qualified to offer advice and comments when they find out you are going to have or may already have had weight loss surgery. Some of the ones I am getting lately are…

  • You’re taking the easy way out.
  • Just diet you can lose the weight if you really wanted to.
  • You just need to join a gym.
  • Take up running.
  • Jenny Craig can help you lose the weight and it won’t cost as much money.
  • Everyone who has the surgery just ends up putting the weight back on.
  • You’re going to regret doing it down the road.

On and on the comments go. Never really offering any true help and not knowing you have already tried most if not all of these suggestions long before you decided to get the surgery.

For many people surgery was not the first option but their last chance to get rid of the extra pounds. It’s not like we are trying to lose 5 or 10 pounds. For many like myself, we are trying to drop 100 pounds or more. We have tried many different diets, joined gyms and tried everything we came across in this endeavor. Maybe in the short term we lost some weight and a few even lost a lot of weight but long term it did not work out.

You may call it being weak willed or not having the strength to stick it out or any of a hundred other excuses but for many of us surgery was the final and best option. Please think before you start offering advice no one asked for. You don’t know what that person has tried before reaching this point.

Negative Calorie Foods


Lately I have been hearing a lot about zero calorie or negative calorie foods. Wikipedia defines Negative Calorie foods as food that requires more energy to digest than the food provides. It's thermic effect or the caloric cost of digesting the food would be greater than its food energy content.

Despite its popularity I have been unable to find a scientific study reporting that any food is calorically negative. (I am not a scientist nor a trained researcher so I may be completely wrong). There are a number of foods however that have been shown to be low calorie foods. Below is a small list I was able to find doing a search on the internet. Some of which I have incorporated into my diet as foods of choice.

Remember this is by no means an exhaustive list and even if considered low calorie, moderation is the key here. Too much of anything is bad for you so consider what you are eating and the quantity.

Produce

  • Celery
  • Romaine Lettuce
  • Cucumber
  • Sugar Snap Peas
  • Carrots
  • Asparagus (My personal favorite)
  • Tomatoes
  • Broccoli
  • Squash

Fruits

  • Peaches
  • 1/2 Grapefruit
  • Strawberries
  • Watermelon
  • Apple
  • Apricots

Drinks

  • Water
  • Decaf unsweet teas
  • Green Tea unsweet
  • Clear chicken, vegetable or beef broth

Dessert

  • Sugar free Jello

Sources

  • Rodale Wellness
  • Health.com

Gym Etiquette

Warning! Rant Incoming


Society is built on rules. Like them or not they are there to give structure and to help keep the peace. It does not matter if it is the Ten Commandments, our nations laws, your company policies or the gym rules. They are all there to help a large group of people to work together without falling into chaos. You don’t have to like them or even agree with them but when you join any group, be it political, social, sport or any organization you can think of, you are introduced to and must agree to follow their rules.

Gyms are the same way. I am a member of a large chain gym and they seem to be exactly what I need at this point in my journey to get fitter. One of the rules is that on the circuit machines do three reps and move on to the next machine. This will free up the machine for others to use. If everyone follows this simple rule every machine in that circuit will free up every few minutes. Doing this allows you to complete the full circuit in approximately 30 minutes. If you want to work on a certain area a little more, re-start your circuit.

Yesterday I was at the gym and had completed my warm-up on the treadmill and went into the room where the circuit machines were located. There are 10 machines in the room and there were two women in there working out. One woman was on machine 2 and the other woman was on machine 9.

No problem I thought, as I was going to start on the 1st machine and work my way around. I finished my three reps on the 1st machine and noticed that the woman was still on machine 2. I went to the cleaning station got some paper towels and disinfectant and cleaned the machine I just finished on. She was still going strong and at this point I counted 5 reps for her. So, realizing she had no intentions of leaving I skipped machines and went on to machine 3. As I was in the first rep the 1st woman got up and left. I finished what I was doing cleaned the machine and moved back to machine 2.

This whole-time woman number 2 was still on machine 9. I didn’t give it much thought as I was several machines to go through before I even got there. As I worked on machine 4, woman number 1 came back into the room headed straight to machine 2 and was back at it.

I worked my way through each machine and 20 minutes later as I got to machine 8, woman number 2 was still at on the same machine. So, I skipped her and did the last machine. I figured she had no intention of finishing any time soon so I head to lockers to get my stuff and head out. Showered, dressed and gym bag in hand I decide to take a quick walk past the circuit room and what do I see. The same 2 women on the same two machines.

People the rules are there for a reason. They help make life work for everyone. Refusing to work within the rules makes life difficult for others and you look like an ass.

OK. Rant complete. Soap box placed back in the closet until the next time it is needed.

01/24/2018

Falling off the wagon


You know how it is. When you first start anything, you are all gung ho about it. No matter if it is a new hobby, a new TV series or dieting. For the first few days or even weeks you are faithful but then something happens. You get distracted and miss a day at the gym. Your company has a shared day at work and gets pizza for everyone. For me it was getting sick for two weeks and not wanting to deal with an illness, dieting and the gym all at the same time. Have you seen those comic strips that makes fun of the macho man who can take anything until he gets a cold and then is all curled up in bed under a blanket crying because he doesn't want to get up? Well that's not me at all. Nope. No way. OK,well maybe a little.

When I get sick I don't want to fool with anything or anyone else. So, for the last two weeks I failed to stay on my diet, did not track any calories or liquid intake and sure as heck didn't go to the gym. So basically, I fell off the wagon.

When things like this happen and they will. Don't let it get to you too badly. Realize what has happened, accept it and the get right back on your routine. If you do so quickly enough you most likely will not have put on much if any extra weight. You can then return to your routine with a new purpose. Don't look at it like a failure but more of a reset. We all need those now and again. A chance to step back and reflect what we have been doing and make any changes we feel necessary.

Do not let a temporary failure set you back. If you let the negatives weigh you down you will never get anywhere and will constantly fail. It's like you used to hear as a kid when learning to ride a bike. If you fall, get up, brush yourself off and get back on.

Two days ago, I restarted my diet and today I am down the two pounds I had put back on. I have my gym bag in the car and today after work, its straight to the gym. I fell down for a couple of weeks but this week I stood up brushed myself off and jumped right back on the wagon.

I spoke with a co-worker who is going through this right now and he told me Sam's has them at a much better price. After doing an internet search the nearest Sam's is 10 miles from my house and you need to purchase a membership. If my math is correct after buying 3-4 12 packs of Premier Protein I will make the money for the membership back in what I save on the price.

Fitbit


Talking to people about the weight I am trying to loose and all the things I am doing, one of the questions I get asked frequently is, Is there any one thing that helps me the most to lose weight? After thinking about it the one best thing that comes to mind is the Fitbit. It was recommended to me by a friend and when I looked into it. It seemed to be an excellent idea. It is a wearable device that looks like a watch. And what it does is that it tracks my steps for the day. For me most importantly it comes with a free app that lets me track calories, steps, water intake, exercise, macros, weight and many other things.

The action of tracking these things makes sure I always know where I am at day-to-day in my progress. I feel that this one item has done more for me staying on top of my diet than anything else. Could I have done it without it? Sure, but it has really made it much easier to keep up with everything I am trying to track.

01/10/2018

My next appointment was on 11/01/2017 with the bariatric clinic. It was a bariatric lifestyle class and was separate from the 6 required visits to the dietitian that insurance requires. I went and was one of two guys in the class and the rest were women. In at least two cases they were there for a second surgery as the first did not work for them. The dietitian giving the class handed out a package that included several samples and a book titled "Bariatric Surgery Handbook". The book touched on things like the different types of bariatric surgeries, the foods and drinks they recommend and food and drinks to stay away from.

A lot of it is common sense but some of it was new information to me. Some example of the common sense information was giving up sodas and all sugary drinks. We also need to give up caffeinated drinks for the rest of our lives. Also, they recommend no alcohol going forward. The reason being is that the majority of the stomach will be removed and so any alcohol will make it to the intestines quicker causing faster absorption. Which is no issue for me as I do not drink anyway.

Other things included in the book are like I said the types of procedures, a FAQ or frequently asked questions section. Nutrition basics, diet progression, the mind-body connection, exercise and common symptoms and side effects.

One of the things the dietitian went over was some of the things we think are healthy but in reality, are not. Included in this are the majority of diet drinks, juices and diet bars. While they are marketed as healthy and or sugar free. Many of the ingredients included in them are anything but healthy. She pointed to a table in the back of the room and said anything we see on it should be avoided. On it were many common items like diet sodas, some juices, various energy bars, and several types of protein drinks marketed for the diet industry. I have to admit this part was an eye opener.

After leaving the lifestyle class I thought it would be best if I went ahead and started on a diet. Even though I had been told by the bariatric surgeon I did not need to. I felt it would be for the best if I did. I did not want to have the surgery and have to start cold turkey as it were on a diet afterward. I started reading and looking up videos on You Tube to get ideas on what I needed to do.

The first thing I did was cut out my morning stop at Whataburger and incorporate a meal replacement shake in the morning. I started with Premiere Protein and it seemed to have worked out really well for me.

01/08/2018

In late September 2017 I went to the doctor for a routine visit and when the nurse weighed me I could not believe how big I had gotten. Talk about a slap in the face, but at 415 pounds this is the largest that I can remember ever being.

Like many people before me, I decided right then to lose weight before I ended up dead from a heart attack or stroke. As I spoke with the doctor that day I broached the subject of weight loss surgery and what his opinion of it was. He was all for it and said he would setup an appointment with a bariatric surgeon he worked with. A couple of days later I received a call from Dr. Malladi's office to setup an appointment/consultation. When the day arrived I was part nervous and part excited to speak with her.

My first appointment with Dr. Malladi was scheduled for 10/18/2017. The day of the appointment a member of her staff took me to a room to weigh me and to ask various questions. I am assuming that this would be similar to any other doctor visit. The nurse sees you for the administrative work and then the doctor will come in for the actual consultation. After being weighed and giving all the information regarding current medications and dosages, current and past medical history and any and all past attempts to lose weight and the results of those attempts, I was then lead to another room to wait for the doctor to see me.

It was not long before the doctor walked in and introduced herself. She went over the information that was collected by the nurse earlier and then asked me what type of weight loss surgery I was considering. By this time I had read many articles and watched numerous videos on You-Tube and had narrowed it down to the sleeve. I told her my choice and asked her opinion as the professional. She told me that I had picked one of the two she would have recommended. The sleeve or bypass(RNY) were her top two choices. I asked about the lap band and she told me that she does not do them any longer as she saw a very low success rate. The only lap band patients she sees anymore were just for maintenance on existing patients.

During my visit with the doctor we also went over insurance requirements. Apparently, my insurance requires a 6 month program where I have to see a dietitian 6 times (once per month), a physiologist once, get blood work done and have a scope down my throat into the stomach looking for issues like a hernia. I was lucky in that I did not have to lose any weight before the surgery as my height and current weight fell within allowed parameters. However, I was instructed not to put any weight on in the next 6 months.

I left the bariatric doctor’s office with instructions not to gain weight and I would be receiving calls from the dietitian’s office, the physiologist’s office and the surgery center to schedule the scope. The blood work I would have to schedule myself but I was to wait until 2 weeks before my next visit with Dr. Malladi.
